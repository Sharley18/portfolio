## Context

Festive periods generated traffic spikes, but campaign planning was previously reactive. Creative approvals were delayed, offer sequencing lacked structure, and post-purchase communication did not fully support retention.

## Scope

- Build a practical festive calendar that connects brand, performance, and operations.
- Coordinate creative timing with audience readiness.
- Reduce decision friction across purchase and repeat-purchase phases.

## Framework used

The plan split the season into three windows: warm-up, peak conversion, and post-peak retention. Each window had a distinct message objective, creative format, and expected user action.

## Execution

### Campaign timing map

Daily and weekly themes were assigned before launch, so ad and content teams were not improvising under peak pressure. Offer depth was staged rather than released all at once.

### Creative and channel roles

Short-form creatives handled attention and urgency, while landing pages and email handled product confidence, delivery expectations, and checkout reassurance.

### Post-purchase continuity

Retention messaging focused on usage ideas, complementary product narratives, and customer support clarity instead of repetitive discount-only communication.

## Outcome signals

Operations teams reported smoother campaign execution and fewer late-stage changes. From a marketing standpoint, user journeys became easier to evaluate because messaging and campaign windows were clearly separated.

## What this case demonstrates

For ecommerce brands, festive growth is not only a media problem. It is an orchestration problem that rewards planning discipline, role clarity, and realistic execution timelines.
