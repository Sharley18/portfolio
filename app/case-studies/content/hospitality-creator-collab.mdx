## Context

The hospitality brand wanted stronger regional visibility through creators, but prior collaborations had inconsistent quality. Some partnerships produced reach without relevance, while others delivered content that could not be reused across owned channels.

## Scope

- Define creator selection criteria tied to campaign objective.
- Build a briefing workflow that protects brand voice.
- Ensure content outputs are reusable beyond one post cycle.

## Strategy

The collaboration model moved from one-off influencer outreach to a scored creator system. Selection considered audience fit, storytelling style, disclosure quality, and prior hospitality content performance.

## Execution

### Creator fit framework

Shortlisted creators were evaluated on audience geography, content tone, and consistency. This avoided over-weighting follower count and improved campaign relevance for Coimbatore-first targeting.

### Briefing and production control

Briefs included narrative direction, non-negotiable brand cues, and practical shoot expectations. Review points were scheduled early so revisions happened before publishing deadlines.

### Reuse plan

Every collaboration had a reuse checklist: short clips for ads, quote snippets for social proof, and image assets for profile refresh. This extended value beyond the initial collaboration window.

## Outcome signals

The team reported better content quality consistency and easier campaign repurposing. Partnerships felt more strategic because deliverables were tied to broader marketing workflows, not just awareness spikes.

## What this case demonstrates

Creator marketing performs best when treated as a content operations channel. Fit, briefing discipline, and post-campaign reuse are the difference between noise and durable brand value.
